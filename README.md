## Overview

The Krasue (Thai: กระสือ; Khmer: អាប, Ahp; Lao: Kasu) is a female spirit of mainland Southeast Asian folklore. By day she lives as an ordinary woman; at night her head detaches from her body and floats free, the heart, stomach and a length of intestine still attached and dangling beneath. In this form she hunts for blood and raw flesh, often preying on livestock or on pregnant women and newborns, and is associated with luminous lights seen drifting across rice fields. The headless body must be hidden during the night and the head must rejoin it before sunrise; if the body is destroyed or hidden too well, the Krasue will die. Belief in the Krasue remains widespread in rural Thailand, Laos and Cambodia. The figure belongs to a wider Southeast Asian complex of detached-head female spirits that includes the Khmer Ahp, Lao Kasu, Malay Penanggal, Indonesian Kuyang and Balinese Leyak, and Filipino Manananggal.

## Etymology

- **Origin**: Thai กระสือ (krasue). The Khmer cognate Ahp is traditionally derived from Sanskrit आप्यति (āpyati), meaning 'to cause anyone to suffer'.
- **Meaning**: The vernacular Thai name has no fixed lexical meaning beyond identifying the spirit; in usage 'krasue' is now generic for this category of detached-head ghost.
- **First use**: Documented in Thai and Khmer folklore long before modern attestation; first systematic English-language description in late 19th- and early 20th-century colonial ethnographies of Southeast Asia.

## Theories & Explanations

### Paranormal / Cryptozoological

**Cursed-woman tradition** _(Thai folk Buddhist tradition, Cambodian Ahp tradition)_

The Krasue is a real woman under a karmic or magical curse — typically for sins committed in a past life, for the practice of black magic that went wrong, or as inherited from a maternal lineage — whose head and viscera detach at night.

### Scientific

**Methane / wetland luminescence hypothesis** _(Various Thai science writers)_

Strange lights reported as Krasue sightings are produced by ignition of methane and other gases released from decomposing organic matter in tropical wetlands and rice fields.

### Skeptical

**Critique of the methane explanation** _(Sirintornthep Towprayoon (KMUTT))_

Associate Professor Dr. Sirintornthep Towprayoon of King Mongkut's University of Technology Thonburi has publicly disputed the methane hypothesis, arguing that methane production over Thai farmland is insufficient for self-ignition and that surface combustion would not produce a freely floating, mobile light.

**Cultural transmission and misidentification** _(Sceptical journalists in Thai media)_

Modern Krasue 'sightings' are explained by a combination of culturally primed misperception, mundane lights (lanterns, vehicle reflections, fireflies, swamp luminescence) and CCTV/camera artefacts in a population in which the figure remains a live cultural reference.
